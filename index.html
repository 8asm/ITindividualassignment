— IBM Global AI Adoption Index 2022

Artificial Intelligence is a powerful technology that can enhance human capabilities, improve efficiency, and solve complex problems. However, AI also poses significant ethical challenges that need to be addressed. In this page, you will find out about three of these challenges: bias, privacy, and manipulation and deception.

Bias refers to the unfair or discriminatory outcomes that may result from AI systems that are not designed or tested properly.
Privacy concerns the protection of personal data and information that may be collected or used by AI systems without consent or transparency.
Manipulation and deception involves the use of AI to influence or deceive people in ways that may harm their interests or autonomy.

These ethical issues are not only relevant for the developers and users of AI, but also for the society and the environment at large.

Bias

Unmasking AI: Unveiling the Bias in Business Algorithms

Back in 1988, a British medical school used a computer program to select applicants for interviews. Upon investigation by the UK Commission for Racial Equality, the program was found to be biased against women and those with non-European names. The reason for that was simple: the algorithm was programmed to mimic already implemented human selection criteria.

Bias poses a significant challenge upon AI systems, regarding how it can have negative consequences for human decisions and trust. It can originate from different factors, such as the data used to train the AI models, the methods used to sample the data, or the choices made by the algorithm designers.
Actions that business leaders can take to reduce bias and ensure fairness in AI include keeping up with the latest research, setting up ethical processes, having evidence-based discussions, exploring how humans and machines can work together, investing more resources in bias research and data, and increasing the diversity of the AI community.
Bias can reinforce existing human prejudices or introduce new ones, resulting in unfair or harmful treatment for certain groups or individuals based on their characteristics or circumstances.

Privacy

Guarding the Gates: Navigating Privacy Concerns in AI Implementation

Key privacy issues arise from AI, such as data quality, data minimization, purpose limitation, consent, transparency, accountability, and fairness.

The ethical issue caused by AI regarding privacy is that AI systems may process personal data in ways that are not transparent, fair, or respectful of the rights and preferences of individuals. AI systems may also pose risks to data security, data accuracy, and data quality, which can affect the trustworthiness and reliability of the outcomes. Moreover, AI systems may generate new or sensitive data that may not be covered by existing privacy laws or regulations, or that may require additional safeguards or oversight. Therefore, privacy professionals need to ensure that AI systems are designed and deployed in a responsible and ethical way that respects the privacy and dignity of individuals and society.
Nevertheless, privacy laws and regulations can indeed apply to AI systems. The EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), for instance, requires data protection by design and by default, data protection impact assessments, data subject rights, and data protection officers.

Manipulation & Deception

Resisting the Dark Side: Countering Manipulation and Deception in AI Systems

Firms operating open-access AI solutions can collect and analyze huge amounts of data from their users, such as their preferences, personality traits, emotions and vulnerabilities.

This information may be used to design and implement strategies that influence the users' decisions and actions in favor of the firms' interests. An example is the case of Cambridge Analytica, which used Facebook data to manipulate voters, and Netflix, which used AI to create personalized trailers for its shows.
Some ways to prevent or mitigate the risks of AI manipulation involve an increase of the transparency and accountability of AI systems and algorithms, regulations over the use and access of data, education and information toward the users about the potential dangers of their online behavior and how to empower them to exercise their rights and choices.
